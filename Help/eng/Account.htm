Chart of Accounts Organization

This part presents the chart of accounts organization and the related information for chart of accounts definition.
Before setting up chart of accounts, it is required to carefully consider the management requirements and study detailed organization and data mining of accounting software.
Management requirements are considered by reports presented to managers, concerned organizations. On the basis of required reports and data mining and processing of accounting software, we will know how to organize chart of accounts to reflect some articles in required reports in the fastest and most convenient mood.
Some notes in chart of accounts organizing:

Currencies

Open sub-accounts of currency accounts to control foreign currencies detailed by currency. Each kind of currency should be controlled by a sub-account.

Bank accounts

Open sub-accounts to control bank accounts by bank. Each bank account should be controlled by a sub-account for convenience to compare with subsidiary books from banks.

Business activities

Chart of accounts must be correctly divided by activity – business, investment or financing activities to have a correct cash flow by indirect method.

Short-term and long-term receivables and payables accounts

Short-term and long-term accounts directly effect on exchange rate revaluation of foreign currency accounts at period-end. Only short-term accounts are created reversing entries of exchange rate difference.

Account Entry screen

Field descriptions

Short Name/ Other Short Name

Short name is account name in brief, used to replace long account names in some reports because of the column width.

Parent Account

Parent account is one-level higher than the current account; used to total up value of sub-accounts, classify and define level of accounts; directly extracted from chart of accounts.

Currency

A kind of currency in which the account balance is controlled. This is a required field and extracted from “Currency List”. If an account has balance controlled by many kinds of currency, it should be split into sub-accounts by currency.

AR/AP Account

For receivables/ payables, accounts are divided into 2 types:

    - 0- “Not AR/AP Account”
    - 1- “AR/AP Account”

AP/AR account is used to control balance detailed by customer, customer or other object.
List of AR/AP accounts is defined in “Accounts Receivable” and “Accounts Payable” parameters.

GL Account

GL account must be the highest level account; supports in account ledger processing. In some summary reports, the amounts of 1-level lower accounts will be totaled up to GL account.

Debit Exchange Rate Type

Used to define the book exchange rate type in debit of foreign currency accounts. It has effect on evaluation of exchange rate difference in period and book exchange rate at period-end.

Credit Exchange Rate Type

Used to define the book exchange rate type in credit of foreign currency accounts. It has effect on evaluation of exchange rate difference in period and book exchange rate at period-end.
