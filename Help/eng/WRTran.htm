“Write-off”

This function is used to delete the pending amount in invoices which have actually not been collected or over paid by customers. This amount reflects incorrectly receivable status.

Note:
it is unnecessary to run this function if you do not control receivables and receipt detailed by invoice. Before running this function, you must declare turnover write-off account and expense write-off account in optional parameters in General Ledger module.

Example: Customer A’s statement on 01/02/2006:

Remaining amount which needs receiving from customer A in receivables list by invoice on 01/02/2006 is: 1 000 + 1 500 000=1 501 000.
Debit balance of customer A in detailed receivables book with account 331 on 01/02/2006 is 1000 – 2 000 + 1 500 000=1 499 000

Closing Dr. Balance: 1 500 000

“Write-off” screen

Field Descriptions

VC Date, VC ID, VC No., Cust. ID, Customer Name, Account

General information of invoices

Amount

Total receivable amount in invoice

Amt. Received

Amount being received at closing of period/year in “Filter” screen

Amt. Receivable

Remaining amount can be negative (-) or positive (+) depending on over or short receiving.

Operations

Select voucher for write-off

To select an invoice for write-off, press “Ctrl + Insert” or click "” - New to create a new detail row, then enter invoice number or click search button.

Note:
All over-paid invoices can be selected for write-off. In case the amount in payment vouchers type 1 is greater than one in invoice, they can also be written off. Click “Extract Data from Overpayments” to select vouchers for write-off.

Create Write-off voucher

Click “Save” to create write-off voucher
