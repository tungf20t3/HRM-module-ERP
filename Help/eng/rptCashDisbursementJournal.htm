Definition

Columns in Cash Disbursement Journal

Data getting way to columns in Cash Disbursement Journal

Credit Account

Account to record credit amount, shown in column 1 in Cash Disbursement Journal

Debit Account 1-4

Accounts corresponding to credit account, these 4 accounts will be shown in 4 columns from 2 to 5 in "Cash Disbursement Journal". Other debit accounts and amount will be shown in column 6 and E.

Debit Accounts Excluded

The accounts to record amount of transactions which satisfy the condition above but do not belong to Cash Disbursement Journal. These transactions are certainly shown in General Journal.
In "Cash Disbursement Journal" definition, "Debit Accounts Excluded" are used to discard the duplicated entries during recording cash receipt/disbursement journal.

For example:
To cash deposit withdrawal transaction, if it has already been recorded in cash receipt journal, it will not be recorded in cash disbursement journal and vice versa. If you decide to record the entry in cash disbursement journal, in cash receipt journal, declare the account 112 - cash deposit in "Credit Accounts Excluded".

Note:
- The accounts above can be defined in lists, separated by comma ",";
- This logic in Cash Disbursement Journal is the same as one in Sales Journal, Cash Receipt Journal and Purchase Journal.
