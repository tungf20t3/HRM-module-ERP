Cash Flow Statement

Filter Condition

Description

The "#" mark in cash flow statement definition

This is used to exclude the corresponding accounts which have been declared in other articles.

For example:
- The aricle "Receipt from Sales, Service Supply" is declared get debit from accounts 111 (cash) and 112 (bank deposit); credit from accounts 511 (sales revenue), 512 (internal sales revenue), 515 (revenue from financing activities), 131 (receivable) and 331 (payable).
- To the article "Other receipt", if you want to declare to get debit from accounts 111 and 112 corresponding to other accounts, which are different from credit accounts defined in the article above, declare "Debit Accounts" as 111,112 and "Credit Accounts" as #;
In period, arising debit of account 111 corresponding to account 511 is VND 10,000,000; one correponding to account 131 is VND 50,000,000; one corresponding to 711 (other revenue) is VND 15,000,000;
Arising debit of account 112 corresponding to account 511 is VND 20,000,000; one corresponding to account 515 is VND 5,000,000; one corresponding to account 711 is VND 10,000,000;
The value of the article "Other Receipt", with credit accounts as #, will be: VND 15,000,000 + 10,000,000 = 25,000,000.
