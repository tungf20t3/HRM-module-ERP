Notes on General Journal Books

General Journal

Print vouchers of special journals

Journal books are actually created on accounting data input in the system. They are typically printed with all accounting transactions. In case of using special journals, it is required to exclude the transactions belonging to these journals from general journals. Therefore, the “Print vouchers of special journals” option is used to generalize these cases. Special journals include: Purchase, Sales, Cash Receipt and Cash Disbursement journals.
These journals can be declared the way to get data to report. This definition is related to argument in general journal creating:

    - General journal must exclude transactions satisfying the definition of special journals;
    - General journal must include transactions satisfying the exclusion of special journals.

Cash Disbursement Journal

Data columns in Cash Disbursement Journal

Definition screen

Credit Account

Account recoded data in credit, shown in column 1 of the journal

Debit Account 1-4

Corresponding accounts to credit account; 4 parameters shown in 4 columns from 2 to 5 in the journals. Amount and other debit accounts will be shown in column 6 and E.

Debit Accounts Excluded

Transactions satisfying the columns above but not belonging to cash disbursement journal. They, of course, will be shown in general journal.

Title 1,2

Column title in Vietnamese and English

Note:
- The accounts above can be declared by list, separated by comma “,”;
- The argument above is the same for Sales, Purchase and Cash Receipt journals.
