Allocation Entry Definition

Path: Modules → General Ledger → Definition → Allocation Entry Definition
At the end of period, accountants usually make following allocation entries depending on business activities of enterprise:

    - Allocate production overhead, direct labor cost to product to calculate unit cost.
    - Allocate sales expenses, business administration expenses to business result account and prepaid expenses account...

In case of management detailed in expense and revenue by business type and department, the quantity of allocation entries may be in the hundreds. Besides, when you need to have data adjusted, it is required to edit allocation entries. So it is waste time if your entries are manually created.
Therefore, AI ERP supports auto allocation entries to reduce working time of accountants and avoid mistakes.
To process the auto allocation, following these steps:

    - Step 1: Define allocation entries - define allocated accounts, accounts receiving allocated amounts as well as the accounts on which the system will base to generate allocation coefficient.
    - Step 2: Create allocation entries - define allocation coefficient in some ways: manually input, system-generate by the coefficient account defined in step 1 or by BOM (or labor cost…). Finally, process the allocation.

In case invoices are allocated to customer, job, manufacturing order…, it is required to declare in allocation entry allocation.

Allocation Entry List

Entry screen

Field descriptions

Ordinal Number

Ordinal number of allocation entries by which the allocation entries will be processed.

Note:
Allocation entry numbers should be set apart from each other some characters in order to insert arising entries during allocation process if necessary.

Description

Description of allocation entry. This is required field and will be used as description in ledger of accounts defined in the allocation entry.

Allocation Type

If allocation type is Debit Account to Credit Account, the double entry of allocation will be Debit on account receiving allocation (Target Account) and Credit on allocated account (Source account).
If allocation type is Credit Account to Debit Account, the double entry of allocation will be Debit on source account and Credit on target account.

Allocation Account

Source account in allocation entry. Although this account is defined as general or subsidiary account, when running allocation, the system will only allocate from subsidiary accounts of a general account.

Receiving Account

Debit account in allocation entry; it is extracted from “Chart of Accounts” and must be sub-account.

Debit Account/ Credit Account (Formula Account)

Accounts used in formula to calculate allocated coefficient. You can define 1 couple of accounts in a formula. It is possible to define only debit or credit account. Then, the system will auto get debit (credit) arising by allocation object in details and group by account in formula.

1. Defind allocation transaction: 632 to 911 by fomular "credit 642 and debit 911 group by jobs".
2. Transaction data:
Debit: 911: 100
Credit: 642: 10 (for job 1)
Credit: 642: 20 (for job 2)
3. Arising amount of account 632: 10.000

When done, allocation result as follow:
Debit: 911: 10.000
Credit: 632: 10.000x10/30 (for job 1)
Credit: 632: 10.000x20/30 (for job 2)

Job/Department/Contract

If the object is checked in allocation account definition screen, the corresponding field will be required.
Allocation entry will be detailed by job (or department/ contract) that is declared in this field. These fields are extracted from corresponding lists and considered as detail allocation objects.

Detail Allocation Entry Screen

This is an optional function to add detail to an allocation entry. However, it’s required to define in allocation entry screen first. It is the same as allocation entry screen.

Add detail and allocation formula

In Allocation Entry screen, click “Detail”

Debit/ Credit Account(s):

    - The “Interest (1/0)” of each detail row helps to define if the value of this record will be positive or negative (plus/minus). This will be added to other records in entry definition to have the final figure to the receiving allocation account.
    - Therefore, some accounts will have negative (minus) value, then allocation value can be decreased or in negative amount if there is no positive (plus) arising amount to balance. The result will be negative value. This is used in case of expense decrease by system-generated formula.
